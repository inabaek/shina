Biography

Clarinetist
Music director
Clarinet teacher

Born in Seoul, Korea in 1987, Shina Kim began playing the clarinet at the age of 13.
Her first teacher was the principal clarinettist of KBS Philharmonic Orchestra in Seoul. In 2004, she moved to Vienna, Austria to pursue her music career. She began studying the clarinet at the age of 16 at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna with Prof. Horst Hajek, who was the principal clarinettist of Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra. During studies, she has gained her experiences by playing in orchestras and chamber music such as ORF radio symphony orchestra, Junge Philharmonie Orchestra, Webern Symphony Orchestra, Max Steiner Film music orchestra and Korean-Austrian Philharmonic Orchestra. She has participated in many music festivals. She was studying piano with Maria Prinz during studying clarinet. She graduated with a Bachelor and Master degree in 2009 and 2011 both with distinctions. After that, she began studying music education and choir conducting at the University of Music and Performing Arts, Vienna with Wolfgang Kornberger and Alois Glaßner. She finished her second bachelor program in 2015. She was a member of Tarqa Wind Quintet and they were invited to Carl Nielsen Chamber music Competition in 2015. Tarqa was a semi-finalist. She has a lot of experience as a chamber music player, such as Clarinet Trio and Quintet, Wind Quintet etc… After she got married in 2017, she and her husband moved to Edinburgh, United Kingdom. Shina Kim was the music director of Bristo Concert Community Band in Edinburgh for a year and she is currently teaching clarinet, piano and music theory at Morningside Music School in Edinburgh since 2018. She has been playing for some music projects and some orchestras in Edinburgh and Glasgow. In early 2020, she has founded a wind quintet in Edinburgh with the musicians from Scottish Sinfonia.
